Course Overview

Complex Analysis (for Chemistry) is a required course for chemistry majors that introduces the fundamental concepts of complex numbers and complex functions. This course bridges the gap between elementary mathematics and advanced topics in theoretical chemistry, particularly quantum mechanics and spectroscopy.

As a teaching assistant, I was responsible for leading discussion sections, grading assignments, and providing one-on-one support to help students grasp challenging concepts such as complex integration, residue theorem, and conformal mapping.

Topics Covered

  • Complex numbers and complex plane
  • Differentiation, the Cauchy-Riemann equation, and analytic functions
  • Integration, Cauchy's theorems and related results
  • Infinite series, Taylor series, and Laurent series
  • The residue theorem and its applications
  • Laplace transform

Teaching Approach

I believe in making abstract mathematical concepts tangible through real-world examples and visual demonstrations. My approach includes:

  • Visual intuition: Using geometric interpretations of complex operations
  • Chemistry connections: Linking complex analysis to quantum mechanics and spectroscopy
  • Step-by-step problem solving: Breaking down complex problems into manageable steps
  • Active learning: Encouraging student participation during discussion sections
  • Individual support: Being available for one-on-one sessions to address specific challenges
