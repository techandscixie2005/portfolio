Course Overview

Mathematical Physics Equations (B) is a core physics course that covers the fundamental partial differential equations (PDEs) and special functions used in physics. The course provides essential mathematical tools for students pursuing advanced studies in theoretical physics, quantum mechanics, and related fields.

As a teaching assistant, I facilitated problem-solving sessions, developed supplementary materials, and provided support to help students master challenging topics including Fourier series, separation of variables, and special functions like Bessel functions and Legendre polynomials.

Topics Covered

  • Chapter 1: Mathematical Physics PDEs (7 hours) - Basic concepts, three types of equations, well-posed problems
  • Chapter 2: Separation of Variables (7 hours) - Method introduction, eigenvalue problems, Sturm-Liouville theorem
  • Chapter 3: Special Functions (8 hours) - Bessel functions, Legendre functions
  • Chapter 4: Integral Transform Methods (3 hours) - Fourier transform, Laplace transform
  • Chapter 5: Fundamental Solutions and Integral Representations (9 hours) - δ function, Green's function
