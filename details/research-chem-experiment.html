Multiscale Molecular Simulation-Driven Physical Chemistry Experiment Teaching Innovation

Fall 2024 - Spring 2025
Competition Project
5th National Undergraduate Chemistry Experiment Innovation Design Competition

Overview

This project pioneered a novel approach to physical chemistry experiment teaching by integrating multiscale molecular simulation with experimental measurements. The goal was to bridge the gap between theoretical concepts and practical laboratory experience, providing students with a deeper understanding of molecular-level phenomena.

Our project focused on measuring the dipole moment of n-butanol/cyclohexane mixtures using both experimental techniques and computational simulations, demonstrating how molecular dynamics (MD) and density functional theory (DFT) can enhance traditional chemistry education.

Computational Framework

We employed a multi-level computational approach:

  • Molecular Dynamics (GROMACS): Simulated solution-phase behavior and molecular interactions
  • DFT Calculations (Gaussian 16): B3LYP/6-31G+g(d,p) level for accurate electronic properties
  • Solvent Modeling: Explicit solvent simulations with appropriate force fields
  • Property Extraction: Dipole moment calculations from charge distributions

Molecular Models

We built two types of molecular models to study concentration effects:

  • High Concentration: 17 n-butanol molecules : 400 cyclohexane molecules (1:23.5 ratio)
  • Low Concentration: 1 n-butanol molecule : 400 cyclohexane molecules (1:400 ratio)
  • Force Field: OPLS-AA for accurate parameterization
  • Simulation Duration: 50ns production runs after equilibration
