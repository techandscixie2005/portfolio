Design and Synthesis of Doublet-Emissive Light-Emitting Materials

Supervisor: Prof. Linsong Cui
Fall 2023 - Fall 2024
Undergraduate Research

Overview

This project focuses on designing and synthesizing novel doublet-emissive organic light-emitting materials. Unlike traditional fluorescence or phosphorescence, doublet emission originates from radical-based excited states and offers unique advantages for organic electronics, including high photoluminescence quantum yields and excellent stability.

Our work centered on the TTM (2,4,5-trimethylphenyl) radical framework, which exhibits strong doublet emission but limited tunability. By coupling TTM with various acceptors, we aimed to redshift the emission while maintaining high quantum yields.

Molecular Design

We designed TTM-AnQ by coupling the TTM radical with 9,10-anthraquinone as the electron acceptor:

  • Donor: TTM radical (strong electron donor, doublet emitter)
  • Acceptor: 9,10-Anthraquinone (strong electron acceptor)
  • Strategy: Donor-Acceptor architecture to tune electronic properties
  • Goal: Redshift emission while preserving doublet character

Synthetic Methods

Multi-step synthesis involving:

  • Friedel-Crafts Acylation: Introduction of acyl groups to aromatic rings
  • Suzuki Coupling: Pd-catalyzed cross-coupling to form C-C bonds
  • Oxidation Reactions: Formation of quinone moiety
  • Purification: Column chromatography and recrystallization

Characterization Techniques

  • UV-Vis Spectroscopy: Absorption properties and band gap determination
  • Fluorescence Spectroscopy: Emission wavelength and quantum yield
  • Cyclic Voltammetry (CV): Redox potentials and HOMO/LUMO levels
  • Transient Fluorescence: Lifetime measurements
  • NMR & Mass Spectrometry: Structural confirmation

Key Insights

  • Stronger electron acceptors lead to greater SOMO stabilization
  • The doublet emission character is preserved in D-A architectures
  • Anthraquinone acceptor effectively reduces SOMO energy
  • Significant redshift enables applications in red OLEDs
