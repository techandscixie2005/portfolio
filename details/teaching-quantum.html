Course Overview

Quantum Physics is a core physics course that introduces students to the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics. This course covers the revolutionary concepts that form the foundation of modern physics, including wave-particle duality, the uncertainty principle, and quantum superposition.

I will be serving as a teaching assistant for this course, demonstrating strong command of theoretical foundations to help students navigate the conceptual and mathematical challenges of quantum mechanics.

Teaching Approach

My approach to teaching quantum mechanics focuses on building both mathematical proficiency and conceptual understanding:

  • Conceptual foundations: Ensuring students understand the physical meaning of quantum phenomena
  • Mathematical rigor: Providing clear derivations and problem-solving techniques
  • Physical intuition: Connecting mathematical results to physical understanding
  • Interactive learning: Encouraging questions and discussion
  • Historical context: Sharing the fascinating story of quantum mechanics development
